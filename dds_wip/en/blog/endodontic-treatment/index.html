Endodontic Treatment: Precision Under the Microscope

Have you ever experienced unbearable dental pain and wondered how to save the affected tooth?

Endodontic treatment, often known as “root canal treatment,” is an essential dental procedure for maintaining oral health and preventing severe complications. Here’s what this procedure involves and why it’s so important for oral health.

What is Endodontic Treatment and When is it Necessary?

Endodontic treatment is a specialized dental procedure that involves removing infected or damaged pulp tissue from inside the tooth. The goal is to save the tooth and prevent the spread of infection.

Pulp infections are often the result of deep or large cavities, dental trauma, and periodontal diseases. These should not be neglected: bacteria that enter the tooth structure through cracks or lesions begin to gradually destroy the dental pulp. This process leads to a series of serious complications, including infection and weakening of the bone, swelling of the ligaments around the tooth, and, eventually, tooth loss.

How Important is Endodontic Treatment?

Preventing Tooth Loss

Without proper endodontic treatment, infection can lead to tooth loss.

Pain Relief

The procedure helps eliminate pain and discomfort associated with pulp infection.

Maintaining Oral Health

A successful endodontic treatment contributes to maintaining a healthy oral environment, preventing further complications.

When is Endodontic Treatment Necessary?

If you’re experiencing one or more of the following symptoms, you may need endodontic treatment:

  • Intense dental pain: if you have dental pain that doesn’t go away with usual pain medications or that gets worse when chewing.
  • Prolonged sensitivity to heat or cold: if your tooth remains sensitive long after the stimulus is removed.
  • Discoloration of the tooth: a darkening or yellowing of the tooth can indicate pulp problems.
  • Swelling and tenderness in the surrounding gums: this can be a sign of infection that has spread beyond the tooth.
  • Pain when touching the tooth: if the tooth is sensitive to pressure.

At Dental Diamonds Studio, we perform endodontic treatments under the microscope, which allows us to see even the smallest details of the tooth’s internal structure, ensuring a successful treatment every time.
